Birria Chili

A weeknight chili built on birria-spiced beef - because birria seasoning (guajillo, ancho, arbol, cumin, Mexican oregano, garlic, a vinegar tang) is already the chili flavor family. Deep, beefy, mildly spicy, protein-dense. Great as tacos and bowls, or Cincinnati-style over protein spaghetti.

Servings4
Calories / serving475
Protein / serving46g
Total1900 kcal · 185g

Ingredients

  • 1 lb prepared beef birria (e.g. Trader Joe's), shredded
  • ~350g leftover cooked 93/7 birria-seasoned beef (optional booster)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 5-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (28oz) Rosa or San Marzano crushed tomatoes (lower acid, slightly sweet)
  • 1/2 to 1 cup beef bone broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Smoked paprika and/or chipotle to taste (season lightly - the beef already carries chile)
  • Cumin and Mexican oregano to taste
  • Pinch of sugar (balances the birria vinegar + tomato acidity)
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: 1 can pinto or black beans
  • To finish: lime, raw diced onion, cilantro

Steps

  1. Warm olive oil in a pot and cook the onion until soft and lightly golden. Add garlic and cook 1 min.
  2. Stir in tomato paste and cook until darkened, about 2 min.
  3. Add the birria beef (and optional ground beef) and stir to combine and heat through.
  4. Add crushed tomatoes and a splash of bone broth. Season LIGHTLY - taste first, the beef already carries chile, cumin, and oregano - then nudge with smoked paprika or chipotle. Add a pinch of sugar to balance the acid.
  5. Simmer low 30-45 min until rich and slightly thickened. If it tastes sharp, simmer longer or add another pinch of sugar (same fix as the butter chicken).
  6. Stir in beans if using and warm through.
  7. Finish with lime, raw diced onion, and cilantro.

Notes

The birria beef is a head start, not a complication - it is the chili flavor family already. Two adjustments because of it: season lighter on top (the beef carries chile/cumin/oregano), and mind the acid (Rosa San Marzano crushed plus a pinch of sugar). Goes Cincinnati-style over Brami protein spaghetti with cheddar and raw onion - that is the chili pasta. Or as tacos/bowl with Mission fiber tortillas. Macros are approximate, estimated from logged portions (a 418g plain bowl ran ~533 cal / ~51g protein / ~8g fiber).
