Saag Chicken

A high-protein take on saag built in one Dutch oven. Marinated chicken gets browned, then the fond becomes the base for onions, ginger, garlic and Indian spices. A full pound of spinach wilts down with broth, then blended cottage cheese gets stick-blended right into the greens for a creamy, protein-loaded sauce — no heavy cream base needed. Holds for days and the macros are excellent.

Servings3
Calories / serving275
Protein / serving43g
Total825 kcal · 130g

Ingredients

  • ~1.5 lbs chicken (breast or thigh), marinated (yogurt or skyr + ginger garlic paste + Indian spices)
  • 1 lb fresh spinach
  • 2-3 white onions, diced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated (or ginger garlic paste)
  • 5-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • Indian spices to taste (garam masala, cumin, coriander, turmeric, Kashmiri chili powder)
  • ~1-1.5 cups chicken broth
  • ~190g cottage cheese, blended smooth
  • 1 tsp heavy cream, to finish
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek), crushed, to finish
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: 1 scoop bone broth powder for extra protein
  • Serve with rice, naan, or paratha

Steps

  1. Brown the marinated chicken in a Dutch oven until it has good color and char in spots. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the fond left behind, cook the onions with a little salt until softened and golden.
  3. Add ginger, garlic, and Indian spices; cook until fragrant, ~2 min.
  4. Add the full pound of spinach and the chicken broth. Close the lid and let it steam and wilt down.
  5. Add the blended cottage cheese (and bone broth powder if using). Use a stick blender to blend everything together and crush the spinach into a smooth saag.
  6. Add the browned chicken back in. Salt to taste.
  7. Finish with a drizzle of cream (~1 tsp) and crushed kasuri methi on top.
  8. Serve over rice, with naan, or with paratha.

Notes

The cottage cheese blended directly into the wilted spinach is the move — it adds body and a big protein boost without the calories of a cream-heavy saag. The fond from browning the chicken is where a lot of the flavor lives, so build the onion base right in it. Full pot runs roughly ~805-850 cal / ~125-135g protein (sauce + chicken, before rice/naan). Makes 3 servings and holds well for 2-3 days. A scoop of bone broth powder pushes the protein even higher. Don't skip the kasuri methi finish — it's what makes it taste authentically saag.
