Sushi Bake Tuna Handrolls

Sushi bake-inspired tuna cooked down with sesame, sriracha, tamari, and tomato, finished with a crispy panko crust and served as seaweed handrolls with chili crunch.

Servings2
Calories / serving260
Protein / serving29g
Total520 kcal · 58g

Ingredients

  • 1 to 2 cans albacore white tuna
  • Sesame oil
  • Sriracha
  • Tamari
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (Red Gold or similar)
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Sesame seeds
  • Kewpie mayo
  • Dried seaweed sheets
  • Crunchy garlic chili oil for dipping

Steps

  1. Cook tuna in a pan with sesame oil, sriracha, and tamari over medium heat.
  2. Add the entire can of diced tomatoes and stir to combine.
  3. Cook down until most liquid has reduced and mixture is thick.
  4. Transfer to a small square sheet tray or oven-safe dish.
  5. Top evenly with panko, sesame seeds, and dots of kewpie mayo.
  6. Bake at 400°F for approximately 15 minutes until top is golden and crispy.
  7. Cut into squares.
  8. Wrap each square in a sheet of dried seaweed to form a handroll.
  9. Dip in crunchy garlic chili oil and eat immediately.

Notes

Originally created while moving out of an apartment using only what was left in the pantry. The diced tomato is the unexpected unlock — it adds acid and sweetness that completely neutralizes the fishiness. The panko crust is non-negotiable for texture contrast.
