Creamed Spinach Saag Pasta

Italian-Indian-Southern fusion: Brami protein spaghetti folded into a creamed-spinach saag-style sauce built on a full pound of spinach, a whole head of garlic, cottage cheese, parmesan, and kasoori methi. Creamed-spinach richness meets saag spice on a high-fiber lupini base.

Servings5
Calories / serving340
Protein / serving17g
Total1700 kcal · 83g

Ingredients

  • 455g cooked Brami protein spaghetti (~207g dry equiv)
  • 1 lb fresh spinach
  • 2 small onions, diced
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 3 tbsp avocado/olive oil blend
  • 25g butter
  • 1/4 cup Mootopia (or other high-protein) cottage cheese, blended smooth
  • 15g shredded parmesan (10g mixed in + 5g to finish)
  • Italian + Indian spices to taste (Italian seasoning/oregano + cumin/garam masala/chili)
  • Kasoori methi (dried fenugreek), crushed, to finish
  • ~150g beef bone broth
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Cook onions in the oil and butter over medium until soft and lightly golden.
  2. Add garlic and ginger garlic paste; cook 1-2 min until fragrant.
  3. Add spinach in batches, wilting it down. Season with the Italian and Indian spices.
  4. Add broth and simmer briefly to meld. Blend smooth with an immersion blender for a saag-style sauce, or leave rustic.
  5. Off high heat, stir in the blended cottage cheese and 10g parmesan so it stays creamy and doesn't split.
  6. Fold in the cooked Brami and toss to coat, loosening with a splash more broth if needed.
  7. Finish with crushed kasoori methi and the remaining 5g parmesan on the plate.

Notes

Fusion of creamed spinach, Indian saag, and Italian pasta. Full pot ~1,700 cal / ~83g protein / ~34g fiber - Brami and the full pound of spinach drive the fiber. Vegetarian as written; folds in shredded chicken beautifully for a protein bump. Kasoori methi is what pushes it from creamed-spinach pasta toward saag - don't skip it. Blend the cottage cheese smooth before adding so it melts like cream instead of going curdy.
