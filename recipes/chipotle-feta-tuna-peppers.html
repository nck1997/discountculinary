Chipotle Feta Tuna Peppers

Charred red bell peppers stuffed with spiced tuna and broiled with Italian cheese. Two proven variants: the original feta + fire sauce version, and a taco-seasoned version with toom and chipotle aioli. Both fast, high-protein, and easy to improvise from pantry staples.

Servings1
Calories / serving350
Protein / serving44g
Total350 kcal · 44g

Ingredients

  • --- ORIGINAL VERSION ---
  • 1 can Safe Catch yellowfin tuna (4.5oz) — strongly preferred over Kirkland albacore for flavor
  • 1 tsp chipotle mayo
  • 3 Taco Bell fire sauce packets (or equivalent hot sauce)
  • 1.5 tbsp low fat feta cheese
  • 1 red bell pepper, halved and deseeded
  • Drizzle olive oil
  • Pinch shredded Italian cheese blend
  • Dried scallions and parsley for finishing
  • Smoked flaky salt to finish
  • Next time: chipotle chili powder + squeeze of lime in the mixture
  • --- TACO VARIANT (3/23) ---
  • 1-2 cans Kirkland albacore white tuna (works fine cooked and seasoned, just more pungent raw)
  • Homemade taco seasoning
  • Splash of beef broth
  • ~1 tbsp Boar's Head chipotle aioli
  • ~1.5-2 tbsp toom (Lebanese garlic sauce)
  • 1 red bell pepper, halved and deseeded
  • Avocado oil spray
  • Pinch shredded Italian cheese blend
  • Mateo's salsa to finish

Steps

  1. Halve and deseed the red bell pepper. Spray or drizzle oil on the skin side.
  2. Char the skin side directly over a burner, under a broiler, or in a toaster oven.
  3. Place charred peppers in a bag or covered bowl to steam for 5-10 minutes. Peel off the skin.
  4. --- ORIGINAL VERSION ---
  5. Mix tuna with chipotle mayo, fire sauce, and feta until combined.
  6. --- TACO VARIANT ---
  7. Cook tuna in a pan with a drizzle of olive oil and taco seasoning. Add a splash of beef broth to loosen and give it a taco bowl vibe. Cook until fragrant and slightly reduced.
  8. Off heat, stir in chipotle aioli and toom until fully combined.
  9. --- BOTH VERSIONS ---
  10. Stuff pepper halves generously with tuna mixture.
  11. Top with a pinch of shredded Italian cheese.
  12. Broil until cheese bubbles and starts to brown.
  13. Original: finish with dried scallions, parsley, and smoked flaky salt. Taco variant: finish with Mateo's salsa.

Notes

Safe Catch yellowfin (39g protein, ~165 cal per 4.5oz can) is clearly the better eating tuna — no fishy smell, could eat it straight from the can. Kirkland albacore (42g protein, ~180 cal per can) is more pungent raw but totally fine once cooked and seasoned. Use Safe Catch when you want to enjoy it; Kirkland when it's what you have. The charred-and-steamed pepper step is the same for both versions — don't skip it. Toom is the move in the taco variant: the garlic richness bridges the chipotle and seasoning perfectly.
