Beef and Broccoli Stir Fry

HEB grass-fed stir fry strips with a tamari-baking soda overnight marinade and a savory Worcestershire-based sauce. Velvety restaurant-style texture from the baking soda tenderize. Finished with scallions over white rice.

Servings4
Calories / serving325
Protein / serving32g
Total1300 kcal · 130g

Ingredients

  • --- MARINADE (overnight) ---
  • 1.21 lbs HEB grass-fed beef stir fry strips
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp tamari
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1.5 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup beef broth (optional — adds depth)
  • --- SAUCE ---
  • 2 tbsp tamari
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce (oyster sauce sub — use 3 tbsp actual oyster sauce if you have it)
  • 1/4 cup beef broth
  • 1 tbsp honey or brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • --- STIR FRY ---
  • 4 cups broccoli florets
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp avocado oil
  • Scallions, sliced, for finishing
  • White rice for serving

Steps

  1. Combine all marinade ingredients, toss with beef, cover and refrigerate overnight. Baking soda is the key to velvety texture — do not skip. Max 24 hours or texture goes mealy. If extending past 24 hours, rinse off marinade and refrigerate plain.
  2. Whisk all sauce ingredients together before anything hits the pan. Have it ready to go.
  3. Blanch broccoli florets in boiling water for 90 seconds, then transfer immediately to an ice bath. Drain and set aside. Keeps it bright green and slightly crisp.
  4. Pull beef from marinade and pat completely dry — twice if needed. Moisture kills the sear.
  5. Heat avocado oil in a wok or large stainless pan over screaming high heat until just starting to smoke. Sear beef in a single layer, 60 to 90 seconds per side. Work in batches — don't crowd the pan. Remove and set aside.
  6. Drop to medium-high. Add garlic and ginger, stir 30 seconds until fragrant.
  7. Pour in sauce mixture and stir — it thickens fast. Add broccoli and toss to coat.
  8. Return beef to pan, toss everything 60 seconds. Serve over rice with sliced scallions on top.

Notes

Grass-fed beef is leaner than conventional and cooks faster — it can get chewy if overdone. Err on the side of undercooking during the sear since it carries over in the sauce. Worcestershire is a solid oyster sauce sub but stronger — 2 tbsp is the ceiling. Add Worcestershire to the sauce only, never the marinade. The baking soda marinade is the single biggest unlock for that velvety Chinese restaurant texture. If the broth got into the marinade by accident (it happens), just make sure you pat the beef extra dry before searing.
