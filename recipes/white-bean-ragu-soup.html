White Bean Ragù Soup

A thick, hearty bean-and-ragù soup that turns leftover ragù into a brand new meal. Cannellini beans add body and a massive fiber boost, beef bone broth deepens it, and a splash of milk tempers the tomato acidity. One of the most macro-efficient meals in the rotation — high protein, high fiber, genuinely satiating. Holds beautifully in the fridge.

Servings2
Calories / serving744
Protein / serving56g
Total1489 kcal · 112g

Ingredients

  • ~625g leftover Turkey Ragù (best with the doubled mixed-meat version: 1lb 93/7 ground turkey + 1lb 90/10 ground beef, all other ragù ingredients doubled — heavier, beefier profile)
  • 425g cannellini (white kidney) beans — full 15oz can, drained and rinsed; mash ~half a cup with a fork for body, leave the rest whole
  • ~315g beef bone broth (Kettle & Fire or similar)
  • Splash of 2% Mootopia milk
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • Various herbs and seasonings to taste
  • Shaved parmesan to finish
  • Optional: fresh lasagna sheets, torn into pieces, for extra carbs/texture
  • Optional: dollop of ricotta on top for richness

Steps

  1. Add ~2 tsp olive oil to the pot and warm over medium.
  2. Add the leftover ragù and warm through.
  3. Pour in the beef bone broth, bring to a simmer, and let it marry 15-20 min.
  4. Stir in the mashed beans early for body, then add the whole beans near the end to warm through.
  5. If using lasagna sheets, tear them into pieces and simmer in the soup until tender, a few minutes before serving.
  6. Finish with a splash of Mootopia milk to add creaminess and temper the tomato acidity.
  7. Adjust herbs and seasoning at the close — taste first, since both the bone broth and the pre-seasoned ragù bring salt.
  8. Top each bowl with shaved parmesan, and a dollop of ricotta if using.

Notes

Built from leftover ragù, so the exact macros shift batch to batch — the numbers here are for the doubled mixed-meat version, beans, broth, oil and parm only (no pasta/ricotta). The beans are the unlock: ~26g of fiber from a single can turns this into a fiber bomb without changing the flavor much. The mashed-then-whole bean technique gives you creamy body plus intact beans for texture. A splash of milk at the end is the move for cutting the tomato acidity. Torn fresh lasagna sheets turn it into more of a lasagna soup if you want the carbs; a dollop of ricotta adds richness and texture. Scales and reheats perfectly — a full pot can carry an entire day's eating. Whole pot (no pasta/ricotta) runs ~1,489 cal / ~112g protein / ~32g fiber.
